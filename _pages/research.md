# Research

Epigenomic regulation is involved in genome architecture, transcription, and metabolism, while closely intertwining with tissue organization. By examining how the epigenome operates at both cellular and tissue levels,  we have developed cutting-edge technologies, including epigenetic MERFISH, enabling subcellular, high-resolution epigenomic profiling. We also developed a high-throughput, imaging-based CRISPR screening platform to pinpoint factors that regulate tissue structure and the three-dimensional epigenome. Moving forward, we will continue developing molecular techniques to study epigenomic mechanisms and use those mechanisms to develop innovative strategies for gene control.

Epigenetic dysregulation is observed in various diseases, including cancer and cardiovascular disorders. Cancer cells often exhibit aberrant modifications that disrupt gene-expression homeostasis and promote malignancy, while in conditions such as atherosclerosis and aortic aneurysms, epigenetics significantly affects vascular smooth muscle function. By elucidating these processes and applying targeted technological interventions, we aim to drive advances in early diagnosis, intervention strategies, and personalized treatments—ultimately leading to more precise and effective medical practice.
